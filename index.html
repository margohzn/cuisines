Cuisines around the world

Tasting and Travelling

Switzerland - Swiss cuisine

Swiss cuisine originates from the Alpine regions of Switzerland, shaped by farming, dairy production, and mountain life. It varies by region (German, French, and Italian-speaking areas), but common ingredients include cheese, potatoes, bread, and cured meats.

Russia - Russian cuisine

Russian cuisine developed in Eastern Europe, shaped by cold winters, long preservation traditions, and influences from Slavic, Tatar, and Central Asian cooking. It relies heavily on preserved ingredients like pickled vegetables, grains, potatoes, and sour dairy products such as sour cream and kefir.

Azerbaijan - Azerbaijani cuisine

Azerbaijani cuisine comes from the South Caucasus region, where Central Asian, Persian, Turkish, and Middle Eastern influences overlap. It is strongly tied to local ingredients like lamb, herbs, rice, pomegranate, and saffron.
